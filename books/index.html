An ongoing series · Set in Harmon

The books.

Each one is a self-contained warning. Read in any order — the cost of stopping is something the protagonists can’t pay either. Published in the order shown.

Book 01

The Good Father

David Marsh · history teacher

A history teacher in a dying town. A promise to a mother in slow decline. A spreadsheet that makes the math work — until it doesn’t. The stranger at the diner names the day the house will go.

“He places the bet anyway.”

Published

Book 05

The Good Brother

Dennis Reilly · county assessor

A senior property assessor. A brother who always knows a guy. Eleven seconds of keyboard. A stranger on the eastbound bus names the audit Dennis has not yet been told to expect.

“Dennis has never found the word that comes before no.”

In writing

Book 02

The Honest Woman

Ruth Calloway · town clerk

Nineteen years in the basement office of Harmon Town Hall. Twenty years of falsified water reports. Six hundred jobs hang on the plant. The woman at the license bureau tells Ruth what filing the truth will cost.

“She files it anyway.”

Ready

Book 09

The Quiet Man

Boyd Haskell · hardware-store owner

Haskell Hardware. Founded 1961. The pencil mark on the door frame is his dead son’s last height. The stranger at the booth names the August the store will close, and the May his wife will leave.

“Closing it means closing the last room where Tyler existed.”

Ready

Book 08

The Good Doctor

Cat Brennan · trauma surgeon

A trauma surgeon and ER physician. A teenage son who is too stable. A mother in slow decline. The promise she made her husband at his deathbed is the standing order she can’t discharge — and the patient she keeps missing is in her own house.

“The patient she keeps missing is in her own house.”
