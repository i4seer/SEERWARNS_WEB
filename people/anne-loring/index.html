Map · People · Anne Loring

Protagonist · The Design Partner

Anne Loring.

Forty-eight. The design half of Loring & Finch, the firm on Washington Street whose ampersand is the marriage of two names. She reads rooms the way she reads blueprints — for what’s plumb, for the load paths, for what fails when the weight exceeds the foundation.

Who she is.

Anne is the one who draws — the trace-paper sketches, the elevations and sections that turn a need into a building. Three design awards; the pediatric hospital wing, with ceilings painted like the sky for sick children, is her proudest work. Her daughter is finishing architecture school and wants to join the firm: the third generation, the inheritance Anne means to leave.

The wound.

Her partner of twenty years, Paul Finch, has skimmed $1.2 million from the budgets of projects they designed together — into an account she didn’t know existed, for a reason she won’t understand until she’s in his kitchen and the gambling receipts are on the table. The firm employs thirty-five people; their paychecks ride on the structure Anne and Paul built. An architect is responsible for what the drawings don’t show.

The warning.

On the courthouse steps, the documents in her hand, a stranger names the bond — report the theft and the professional-liability bond is pulled, the projects halt, the thirty-five go home with paychecks that may not clear, the wing with her name on it gets a lawsuit attached. Paul’s reputation dies with disclosure; hers is the one that has to survive, because her reputation is the firm and the firm is the thirty-five families.

What she chooses.

Anne files the audit — the honest structural act — and pays the price. The firm survives, smaller and scarred; the partnership and the friendship do not. By spring the brass plate reads Loring Architecture. One name on the door, the ampersand retired. She kept the structure standing by tearing into its facade, and learned that the foundation she was responsible for was the person she trusted.

Where she fits.

The case runs through the Harmon County DA’s office under Rachel Thornton, assigned to forensic accountant Maria Gutierrez — the same Maria who caught the buried date in The Loyal Man. The courthouse steps are the series’ recurring stage, also Rachel Kim’s in The Reluctant Witness; and Paul’s gambling-driven theft rhymes with The Good Father’s good man undone by a bet.
