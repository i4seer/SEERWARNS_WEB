Map · People · Claire Novak

Protagonist · The Good Writer

Claire Novak.

A novelist who became famous on a memoir of her mother’s death, true, and hers to tell, except it was her sister’s too, and she told it without asking. She came to Harmon to escape the fallout.

Who she is.

Claire turned her mother’s decline into a bestseller, the betrayal of a family’s privacy that made her name and cost her a sister. She moved to Harmon to hide and found, in the small lives around her, the raw material a writer cannot stop seeing.

The conceit.

Within the world of the series, Claire has read all nineteen prior Seer Warns books and reviewed the first, The Good Father, for the Harmon Sentinel. So the Seer is, to her, both a real figure in the town she half-believes in and a literary franchise she has built a career reading. The series, in her, folds in on itself.

The warning.

A stranger at a reading, and again at Roasters, the Main Street coffee shop now “under new management”, names the next book Claire will write: the one about Harmon, mining the people who trusted her, too true to deny. And she tells Claire about the Seer, the pattern of strangers and prophecies and wrong choices, and that the book Claire writes about it will be this book.

The outcome.

The book you are holding. The Good Writer is written to be read last, the volume in which the Seer is revealed to be both a figure in Harmon and a published franchise a character has read, the series turning to look at itself in the moment of its own making. Claire receives the warning the way every protagonist before her did, and makes her choice the way they all did. You were warned.

Where she fits.

She touches every book, that is her design. She read the nineteen; she reviewed The Good Father; the people she mines are the protagonists of every other volume. Her Seer scene is at Roasters, the shop whose slow decline across the later books is the visible weathering of the one town all twenty stories happen in.
