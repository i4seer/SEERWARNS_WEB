Map · People · Diana Torres

Protagonist · The Lawyer Daughter

Diana Torres.

Forty-three. Corporate attorney, the daughter who left Harmon and succeeded. The executor, the planner, the one who calls everyone on their birthday, the one who holds the family together. Until she finds the drawer her father left locked.

Who she is.

Diana does mergers and acquisitions for a firm on Washington Street, near the courthouse. She built her whole identity on being the dutiful daughter, and on having gotten out, made it, become “the successful one,” which carries its own guilt: she wasn’t in Harmon when her father got sick. She is married to Marcus, an architect, with two children, Sofia and Mateo. Her father Ernesto was her hero.

The wound.

Ernesto Torres was the immigrant who built Torres Construction from nothing and a brick house on Riverside Avenue in the North End, the family hero, the man everyone believed in. Six weeks after his funeral, cleaning out the estate, Diana finds a locked drawer: forty years of cancelled checks, $1,200 a month, to a woman named Carmen Reyes in Bridgeport, and a half-sister, Luz, born thirteen months after Diana, abandoned and never spoken of. Her father kept a second family for forty years, five hours north.

The warning.

A stranger at the funeral reception names, before she has even opened the drawer, the shape of the choice waiting in it: whichever she chooses, honor the secret or expose it, she loses the family she has spent her life holding together. There is no version that keeps it whole, because it was two families all along.

What she chooses.

Diana drives the road her father drove four hundred and eighty times, Route 11 north, through the lake tunnel, five hours into pine-and-birch country, and chooses the truth. She finds Carmen and Luz; she brings the second family into the light; and she pays the price the stranger named. She gains a sister. She loses the unbroken version of her father and the role she organized her life around. The good daughter learns the dutiful thing and the honest thing were never the same errand.

Where else she appears.

The Torres family is a North End family, and the book threads the town through it: Dennis Reilly does the probate valuation; Maggie Caldwell (The Loyal Daughter) is at the funeral; the county forensic accountant Maria Gutierrez is a Gutierrez niece; and the Harmon High principal sits on the Torres scholarship committee.
