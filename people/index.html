The residents of Harmon

The people.

One protagonist per book, and the families and neighbors who orbit each one. The cast accumulates as the series grows, the ER nurse you meet in book one is still on shift when the burning-out doctor comes through years later; the careful county assessor who appears at a clerk’s basement window has his own book waiting. Everyone here lives in the same town. Below: the twenty whose turn it is to take the booth, then the recurring faces who carry the town between them.

The Marsh family.

From The Good Father. The first family the series follows, and the family it uses to set its terms. Four people at a breakfast table; the book counts the plates as they disappear.

  • David Marsh · history teacher, Harmon High, Room 217
  • Sarah Marsh · ER nurse, St. Clare’s, the recurring hospital face (also books five and beyond)
  • Emma Marsh · daughter, fifteen, the one who notices first
  • Helen Marsh · David’s mother, Lakeview Memory Care, Room 14B
  • Cooper Marsh · son, nine, Little League at Elm Street Park

The Calloway family.

From The Honest Woman. The clerk who keeps the record, and the family the plant fed for three generations, the family whose name is on the plant.

  • Ruth Calloway · town clerk, nineteen years in the basement office
  • Diane Calloway-Park · Ruth’s sister, hairdresser on Main Street
  • Tom Park · Diane’s husband, twenty-two years on the plant kill floor
  • Chester · Ruth’s golden retriever, asleep under the Town Hall desk

The Haskells.

From The Quiet Man. A store, a marriage, and a pencil mark on a door frame.

  • Boyd Haskell · owner, Haskell Hardware, thirty-six years behind the counter
  • Jean Haskell · his wife, retired elementary-school secretary
  • Tyler Haskell · their son, who died at eleven, the door frame is his last height

The Reilly brothers.

From The Loyal Man. The careful brother who keeps the honest books, and the generous one who keeps the favors.

  • Dennis Reilly · senior county property assessor, sixteen years at the same desk
  • Kevin Reilly · his brother, laundromats, storage units, the cosigned mortgage, the Lexus
  • Pam Reilly · Dennis’s wife, fourth-grade teacher at Harmon Elementary

The Brennan household.

From The Tired Mother. A doctor who can read a whole town, and the patient she keeps missing in her own house.

  • Cat Brennan · ER physician, St. Clare’s
  • Miles Brennan · her son, too quiet, too stable
  • Ray Brennan · her late husband, whose deathbed standing order she can’t discharge
  • Gloria · Ray’s mother, in slow decline out in Marl

The Torres family.

From The Lawyer Daughter. The North End family that built much of the North End, and the drawer the father left locked.

  • Diana Torres · corporate attorney, the good daughter
  • Ernesto Torres · her late father, founder of Torres Construction
  • Rosa Torres · his widow, still in the brick house on Riverside Avenue

The figure who is in every book.

Not a person on this list, and yet the one who appears in all twenty. The Seer, the calm stranger with the still hands and the brown pencil, sits down across from each protagonist exactly once, and is glimpsed in the background of every other story: a man in the corner booth of the Lamplighter, a passenger on the Route 22 bus, a figure at the end of the marina dock.
