The residents of Harmon

The people.

A protagonist per book, plus the families and neighbors who orbit each one. The cast accumulates as the series grows — the surgeon you meet in the background of book one is the one the stranger sits down across from in book eight. Listed by the book in which they take the booth.

The Marsh family.

From The Good Father, book one. The first family the series follows, and the family the series uses to set its terms. Four people at a breakfast table; the book counts the plates as they disappear.

  • David Marsh · history teacher, Harmon High, room 217
  • Sarah Marsh · ER nurse, St. Clare’s Hospital, nights
  • Emma Marsh · freshman, Harmon High, the one who notices first
  • Helen Marsh · mother, Lakeview Memory Care, Route 9

The Calloway family.

From The Honest Woman, book two. The town clerk and the foreman who has been on a list since before either of them knew there was a list. Pages forthcoming with the book’s release.

The Reilly brothers.

From The Good Brother, book five. Dennis the careful older brother, the county assessor; Marie the sister who calls the attorney general; their mother in a kitchen on Oak Street who never understands why the boys stopped coming to dinner together. Pages forthcoming.

The Haskells.

From The Quiet Man, book nine. Boyd, who owns the hardware store, and Jean, who has been waiting nineteen years for Boyd to take the pencil mark off the door frame. Pages forthcoming.

The Brennan household.

From The Good Doctor, book eight. Cat the trauma surgeon, her son who is too stable, and a husband whose deathbed standing order is still being filled in the surgeon’s head. Pages forthcoming.
