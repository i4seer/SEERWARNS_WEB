Map · People · Rachel Kim

Protagonist · The Reluctant Witness

Rachel Kim.

A software engineer — a woman whose job is precision. She saw the accident at Fifth and Harmon exactly, the way her memory records everything. The truth is already hers. The swearing is what costs.

Who she is.

Rachel’s memory has the precision of a profession built on precision. She witnessed the wreck at the intersection of Fifth and Harmon — the car that ran the red, the whole sequence — with the certainty of a person who cannot un-see what she saw.

The wound.

The court subpoenas her account. Raymond Doyle is the man who ran the light — a local man, with a family, whose insurance and future and freedom depend on the thing she saw not being true. The town has a long memory and a clear sense of its own.

The warning.

A stranger in a Ferry Street parking lot names the trial, the testimony, the day she gives it, and the way the town files her afterward — the cost that the truth itself never warned her about.

What she chooses.

Rachel takes the stand and swears to what she saw. The truth holds; the town does not forgive her for it. She keeps her precision, her record, her certainty — and loses the easy belonging of a person the town counts as one of its own. The witness learns that being right and being welcome were never the same thing.

Where she fits.

The case runs through the Harmon County DA’s office under Rachel Thornton, with Assistant DA Marcus Webb — the same shared DA’s office that handles The Design Partner. Her plight — right, and punished for it by her own town — rhymes with Ruth Calloway’s and Jack Callahan’s.
