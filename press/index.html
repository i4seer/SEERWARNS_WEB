For reviewers, educators, & rights

Press.

Review copies, interviews, classroom adoption, foreign rights, and the standing list of what the imprint can and can’t send. Direct correspondence to press@i4seer.com.

Review copies.

Galleys of The Good Father are available, in print and electronic editions, to recognized critics, working booksellers, and educators considering classroom use. Requests should include the publication or institution, the kind of coverage planned, and an address that takes a small parcel. The imprint sends without further follow-up; reviewers are not expected to confirm receipt or to file by a date.

Galleys of The Honest Woman, book two, are available beginning in early 2027 ahead of the standard publication window. Requests for later volumes are kept on a list maintained by the press; the list is not pruned, and reviewers who have requested earlier galleys will be approached for later ones unless they ask otherwise.

Interviews.

The author is reachable through the imprint at authors@i4seer.com. Interviews are conducted by email or by recorded video call. The author does not appear in person except by prior arrangement at independent bookstores in the American Midwest. The press maintains, by quiet preference, no list of upcoming appearances; one or two events per year are listed on the about page in the season they occur.

Classroom & library.

The series is available at standard institutional discount through the usual channels. A short teacher’s companion to The Good Father, ten pages, chapter-by-chapter discussion questions, no thematic prescription, is available as a PDF on request. Booklists for classes that have adopted the series are read with interest; the press does not publish them.

Foreign rights.

World rights are held by the imprint. Translation inquiries should include the proposed language, the publisher of record, the projected publication window, and any standing relationship with the series. The imprint has not, to date, declined a translation request from a serious publisher and does not anticipate doing so.

Quotation.

Brief quotation from the books, in the manner standard to reviewing and criticism, is welcome without further permission. Longer reproduction, including reprint anthologies, classroom course packs distributed for fee, or any quotation that constitutes a meaningful fraction of a chapter, requires written permission from the imprint and is, in most cases, granted.

The thing the press will not do.

The imprint does not confirm or deny that any place named in the books is a real place. The imprint does not confirm or deny that any character is based on a real person. The imprint does not respond to inquiries that begin with a claim of having been there. These are not editorial preferences; they are conditions of the work, which is fiction.
